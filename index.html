Vibrant Colors and Strange Animals

Some species, like clownfish, are born male but can change into females, and this sex change is irreversible. Saltwater fish are very commonly kept in aquariums for entertainment. Many saltwater fish are also caught to be eaten, or grown in aquaculture. However, many fish species have been overfished and are otherwise threatened by marine pollution or ecological changes caused by climate change.

Spotlighted Creatures

Mandarin Dragonet - Dragonets have special feeding requirements and should be housed in an established aquarium with large amounts of live rock and sand.

Ocellaris Clownfish - The classic clownfish that everyone has grown to love, Nemo! The Ocellaris Clownfish may be one of the aquarium industry's most popular marine fish.

Yellow Tang - The reef-dwelling yellow tang is known for its bright yellow body. It is a popular aquarium fish and is exported from Hawaii in great numbers for this purpose.

Seahorse - Unique saltwater fish with horse-like heads, known for their upright swimming posture, male gestation, and poor swimming skills, relying on their dorsal fin for movement.
